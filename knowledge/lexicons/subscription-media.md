# Subscription Media Lexicon

Word choice for streaming/news/content-subscription CRM copy. The best message in this sector doesn't feel like marketing at all — it feels like the service working: "yeni bölüm yayında", "the next episode is up". Lead with the content, not the platform; name titles, not features. Personalization language should signal *taste* ("izlediklerine göre") rather than surveillance ("we noticed you watched..."), and it must be backed by real viewing/reading data — fake "picked for you" is worse than no personalization. FOMO abuse ("everyone's watching!", "don't miss out!") is the sector's junk food: short-term clicks, long-term unsubscribes.

## Use / avoid

| Use | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "yeni bölüm yayında" / "new episode is out" | "kaçırma!", "herkes bunu izliyor" / "don't miss out!", "everyone's watching" | The content drop IS the news; FOMO framing cheapens it |
| "{{series_name}} devam ediyor — kaldığın yerden" / "pick up {{series_name}} where you left off" | "içerikler seni bekliyor" / "so much content awaits" | Specific title + position beats vague abundance |
| "izlediklerine göre şunu sevebilirsin" / "based on what you watch, you might like" (with real data) | "sana özel seçtik" with no viewing history behind it | Taste language must be earned by data or it reads as spam |
| "deneme sürümün 3 gün sonra bitiyor" / "your trial ends in 3 days" | "son şansın!", "hemen harekete geç" / "last chance!", "act now" | The date is the urgency; theatrics erode trust before billing |
| "10 dakikan var mı? Tam sana göre bir bölüm" / "got 10 minutes? there's an episode that fits" | "binlerce saat içerik" / "thousands of hours of content" | Meet the user's moment; catalog-size brags create choice paralysis |
| "ödemeni alamadık — kartını güncelle" / "we couldn't process your payment — update your card" | "hesabınız askıya alınacaktır!" / "your account will be suspended!" | Payment recovery is service, calm and factual; threats accelerate churn |
| "{{title}} 31 Mayıs'ta kataloğdan ayrılıyor" / "{{title}} leaves on 31 May" (if a real licensed exit) | "yakında gidiyor, acele et" / "leaving soon, hurry" | Real dates are useful information; vague deadlines are manipulation |

## Urgency rules

Urgency is allowed only for **real, dated expirations**: trial end date, plan-price change date, licensed content leaving the catalog on a confirmed date. State the date; never add pressure decoration ("hurry", "last chance", countdown timers). Consumption nudges ("come back and watch") get **zero** urgency — a lapsed viewer pressured is a canceled viewer. Never imply content scarcity that doesn't exist; streaming catalogs don't "sell out".

## Tone calibration by journey stage

| Journey type | Tone shift |
|---|---|
| Welcome / onboarding | Enthusiastic host: help them find the first great thing fast; one strong recommendation beats a catalog tour |
| Trial conversion | Value recap in *their* terms ("bu hafta 4 bölüm izledin") + plain plan facts; no pressure theater |
| Engagement / new-content drops | Pure editorial: title, one-line hook, play. The recommendation is the whole message |
| Churn prevention (consumption drop) | Warm, zero guilt: "senin için yeni bir şey var" — never "seni özledik, neden gittin?" |
| Payment failure | Calm, transactional, solution-first; no marketing content at all (keeps transactional status) |
| Winback | Honest + concrete: what's new since they left, in their taste profile; light self-awareness ok, no desperation |

## TR / EN notes

- TR: "sen" fits entertainment/streaming; news and finance-adjacent publications often need "siz" — resolve at intake, never mix within a brand.
- TR: write native recommendation phrasing ("tam senlik", "kaldığın yerden") — not calqued EN ("senin için elle seçildi" reads translated).
- TR: content titles stay in original language if that's how the platform lists them; don't translate series names.
- EN: sentence case everywhere including subjects; editorial voice means specific verbs ("returns", "premieres", "drops") over hype adjectives.
- Both: genre words carry taste signals — use the user's actual genres from data, not aspirational ones.

## Banned outright

"Herkes bunu konuşuyor" / "everyone's talking about it" (unverifiable), "kaçırma!" / "don't miss out", "son şans" / "last chance" (except a real dated expiry, stated as the date), "binge" as an imperative in TR copy, "sınırsız eğlence dünyası", guilt formulations ("seni özledik ama sen bizi özlemedin galiba"), countdown timers on anything except a real trial/billing date, "ÜCRETSİZ" in caps, more than one exclamation mark per message.
