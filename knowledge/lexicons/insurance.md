# Insurance Lexicon

Word choice for a product people buy hoping never to use. The governing rule: **describe coverage and process, never predict or dramatize the event it protects against.** You may say what a policy *covers* and what a claim *process* looks like; you may not imply an accident, illness, theft, or disaster is imminent to motivate a purchase or renewal. Fear-based urgency is both a well-documented conversion-killer in this sector (it reads as manipulative, not persuasive) and a conduct-rule exposure in most markets — assume a regulator equivalent to Türkiye's SEDDK applies. The second rule, specific to this sector: **never promise a claim outcome.** Whether a specific claim is paid, and how much, depends on policy terms and case facts that marketing copy cannot know in advance — "we'll take care of you" is fine; "your claim will be paid" is not.

## Use / avoid

| Use | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "teklifiniz 48 saat geçerli" / "your quote is valid for 48 hours" | "bu fiyatı bir daha göremezsiniz!" / "you'll never see this price again!" | Factual validity window vs manufactured scarcity |
| "poliçeniz [tarih] tarihinde sona eriyor" / "your policy expires on [date]" | "yarın kazaya uğrarsanız ne olur?" / "what happens if you crash tomorrow?" | Real calendar fact vs fear-based hypothetical |
| "hasar sürecinizi uygulamadan takip edebilirsiniz" / "you can track your claim in the app" | "hasarınız garanti ödenir" / "your claim is guaranteed to be paid" | Process description vs outcome promise no one can make in advance |
| "kapsamınızı gözden geçirebilirsiniz" / "you can review your coverage" | "bu eksiklikle büyük risk alıyorsunuz" / "you're taking a huge risk with this gap" | Informational nudge vs alarmist framing of the customer's own choices |
| "taksit ödemeniz gerçekleşmedi; kartınızı güncelleyebilirsiniz" / "your installment payment didn't go through; you can update your card" | "poliçeniz iptal edilmek üzere!" / "your policy is about to be cancelled!" | Neutral logistics vs alarm language that trains users to ignore real notices |
| "hasarınız için yanınızdayız" / "we're here for your claim" | "hayatınızın en kötü gününde bile size güveniyoruz" / "even on the worst day of your life, trust us" | Supportive plain statement vs exploiting a hypothetical tragedy for copy |
| "ihtiyacınıza göre ek teminat ekleyebilirsiniz" / "you can add coverage based on your needs" | "bu paketi almazsanız pişman olursunuz" / "you'll regret it if you don't buy this package" | Feature/option framing vs regret-based pressure |

## Urgency rules

Default: urgency is **allowed only on real, system-known dates** — renewal date, grace-period end, a document deadline, a quote's true expiry. State the date once, neutrally, no exclamation marks: "poliçeniz 12 Ağustos'ta sona eriyor" not "SÜRENİZ DOLUYOR!". Never attach urgency to an imagined future event (accident, illness, theft, disaster) — that is fear-selling, the sector's single most damaging pattern, and it degrades trust in every message that follows it, including genuine claims communication.

## Tone calibration by journey stage

| Journey type | Tone shift |
|---|---|
| Quote abandonment | Helpful continuation, zero pressure: the quote is saved, here's what it covers, one click to finish |
| Welcome / policy activation | Reassuring and procedural: what's covered, how to reach support, how to file a claim — said once, clearly, before it's ever needed |
| Payment recovery | Neutral logistics, fix-first, no alarm vocabulary — most failures are expired cards, not disputes |
| Pre-renewal | Confident and factual: what changed (if anything), the real date, the real price; earn the renewal on service and clarity, not fear of lapsing |
| Post-claim (paid/resolved) | Warm and brief — the claim itself was the hard part; don't over-narrate a good outcome |
| Post-claim (rejected/partial) | Careful, human-escalation-first; a rejected claim is never the moment for automated marketing tone — route to support/negative-signal suppression |
| Cross-sell | Educational, needs-based: "here's a gap in your coverage" framed factually, never "here's what could go wrong" |
| Winback | Factual news (new products, digital claims features) delivered plainly; no "we miss you" sentimentality, no re-litigating why they lapsed |

## TR / EN notes

- TR: "siz" without exception — insurance sits squarely in the locale overlay's finans/sağlık "siz" bucket regardless of product line or customer age.
- TR: claim/coverage terminology must be exact and match the actual policy wording — "teminat", "muafiyet", "hasar", "tazminat" used loosely or interchangeably creates real confusion about what is and isn't covered.
- Both languages: never state a coverage amount, deductible, or claim outcome that isn't pulled from the actual policy/claim record — a wrong number here is a trust and possibly regulatory problem, not just a copy error.
- Numbers: premium, deductible, and payout figures always exact and sourced from system data; never round in a direction that flatters the product.

## Banned outright

"kesinlikle ödenir" / "guaranteed to be paid", "hiçbir zaman reddedilmez" / "never rejected", any implied guarantee of claim approval or amount, fear-based hypotheticals about accidents/illness/disaster/theft used to motivate purchase or renewal ("ya yarın...", "what if tomorrow..."), fabricated urgency on renewal or quote validity, "en ucuz sigorta" / "cheapest insurance" style unverifiable superiority claims, exclamation marks in anything about claims/payment/coverage status, emoji in claim or payment contexts, minimizing language about exclusions ("küçük bir şart" for a material exclusion).

## Regulated context

`regulated: true` binds the copy pipeline beyond this file's own rules: any claim-outcome or coverage-scope statement is stamped `⚖️ legal review required` by copy-reviewer before it ships — manual sign-off, no exceptions. Fear-based framing detected in any draft forces a rewrite pass, not a warning-and-ship.
