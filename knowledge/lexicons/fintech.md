# Fintech Lexicon

Word choice for financial products, where copy carries regulatory exposure. The governing rule: **describe capability, never predict outcome.** You may say what the user *can do* ("you can set up a recurring deposit"); you may never say what they will *gain* ("grow your money", "beat inflation", "don't miss this opportunity"). Urgency applied to a financial decision is prompting, and prompting investment behavior invites regulatory action (SPK/BDDK-style conduct rules in Turkey; equivalent marketing-conduct regimes exist in most markets — assume one applies). Calm precision is also the conversion strategy: people move money to products that sound like institutions, not like promotions.

## Use / avoid

| Use | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "hesabınız kullanıma hazır" / "your account is ready to use" | "kazanmaya başlayın!" / "start earning today!" | Capability statement vs implied return promise |
| "kimlik doğrulamanız için son adım: belge yükleme" / "one step left in your verification: document upload" | "hemen tamamlayın, fırsatları kaçırmayın" / "complete now before you miss out" | Procedural clarity converts; urgency on finance is a conduct violation |
| "yatırım araçlarını inceleyebilirsiniz" / "you can explore the available instruments" | "şimdi yatırım yapmanın tam zamanı" / "now is the perfect time to invest" | Market-timing language = investment advice |
| "para transferi ücreti: 0 TL" (if factual) / "transfer fee: €0" | "tamamen ücretsiz, hiçbir gizli maliyet yok!" / "totally free, no hidden costs!" | State the fee schedule; absolute claims invite scrutiny |
| "geçmiş performans gösterge niteliğindedir, garanti değildir" / "past performance is not a guarantee of future results" | omitting risk language where products are named | Risk disclosure is mandatory context, not optional fine print |
| "otomatik birikim talimatı oluşturabilirsiniz" / "you can set up an automatic savings plan" | "geleceğinizi güvence altına alın" / "secure your future" | Feature description vs outcome promise |
| "ödemeniz gerçekleşmedi; kartınızı güncelleyebilirsiniz" / "your payment didn't go through; you can update your card" | "hesabınız risk altında!" / "your account is at risk!" | Alarm language in finance reads as a scam and trains users to ignore real alerts |

## Urgency rules

Default: **no urgency, ever.** Scarcity, countdowns, "last chance", "limited spots", and any time pressure attached to depositing, investing, trading, or borrowing are banned without exception — this is a regulatory line, not a style preference. The only permissible deadline language is procedural fact the system knows: an ID document expiring, a verification session timing out, a regulatory or fee-schedule change with an announced effective date. State such dates once, neutrally, with no exclamation marks ("belgenizin geçerlilik süresi 15 Mart'ta doluyor" / "your document expires on March 15"). Never attach a deadline to a market condition.

## Tone calibration by journey stage

| Journey type | Tone shift |
|---|---|
| Welcome / KYC rescue | Patient, procedural, reassuring: name the exact step, why it's needed, how long it takes; treat verification as normal, not as the user's failure |
| Activation (verified → funded) | Purely enabling ("hesabınız hazır; ilk para yatırma işleminizi uygulamadan yapabilirsiniz"); zero opportunity framing |
| Churn prevention | Quiet utility check-in — surface a useful feature or a factual account summary; never mention their balance activity in a way that pressures action |
| Payment failure | Neutral logistics, fix-first, no alarm vocabulary |
| Feature adoption / cross-sell | Educational register: explain what the product is and its risks, invite exploration; every named investment product carries risk language |
| Winback | Factual news (new capabilities, reduced fees) delivered plainly; no incentives without compliance sign-off, no "come back" emotional appeals |

## TR / EN notes

- TR: "siz" without exception, including young-audience trading apps — money changes the register. Full sentences, no fragments.
- TR: regulatory vocabulary must be exact — "yatırım tavsiyesi değildir" where instruments are discussed; align terminology with SPK/BDDK-conformant language your compliance team uses; never translate risk disclosures ad hoc — use the approved wording.
- EN: equivalent discipline — "this is not investment advice", "capital at risk" where required; no contractions in risk or account-status copy.
- Numbers: always exact and sourced from system data (fees, rates, dates); write currency and rates in the account's locale format; never round in a direction that flatters the product.
- Both languages: name the sender clearly and consistently — finance copy that looks even slightly off-brand gets reported as phishing.

## Banned outright

"guaranteed returns" / "garantili getiri", "risk-free" / "risksiz", "kazanç fırsatı" / "profit opportunity" / "earning potential", "kaçırmayın" / "don't miss out", "şimdi tam zamanı" / "now is the time", "beat the market" / "piyasayı geride bırakın", "make your money work for you" / "paranız sizin için çalışsın", "paranızı katlayın" / "double your money", any specific or implied return figure not contractually fixed, countdowns or timers on any financial action, "son şans" / "last chance", "herkes alıyor" / "everyone's buying", FOMO framing of market moves, exclamation marks in anything mentioning money or account status, emoji in investment contexts, alarmist account language ("hesabınız kapatılacak!") outside genuine regulatory notices.
